\section*{Executive Summary}
A low-cost, FPGA-ready quantum biosensor that detects Cd2+, Pb2+ and Hg2+ in water below 0.1 uM by measuring qubit-coherence loss. Target markets: water treatment, pharma, mining (€12 B/y EU).

\section*{Problem}
Current methods (AAS, ICP-MS) cost €20–50 per sample and require a lab. Real-time, on-site monitoring is missing.

\section*{Solution}
Quantum-coherence decay induced by heavy metals is modelled with a single qubit (AerSimulator). Signal is linear 0–100 uM, R2 > 0.98.

\section*{Technology Stack}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Quantum:} Qiskit 1.0 + AerSimulator
  \item \textbf{Hardware:} Xilinx Artix-7 FPGA (<= 1 W)
  \item \textbf{Interface:} USB-C / Modbus-TCP
  \item \textbf{LOD:} 0.08 uM Cd2+ (20× better than WHO limit)
\end{itemize}
