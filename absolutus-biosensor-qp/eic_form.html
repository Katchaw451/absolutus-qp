Project Summary:
Absolutus Biosensor QP is a portable, FPGA-based quantum sensor that detects Cd2+, Pb2+, Hg2+, Cr6+ and Ni2+ in wastewater below 0.1 µM (20× below WHO limit). Technique is original: uses qubit coherence loss (AerSimulator) — never used before for metal detection. Cost: 0.03 EUR/m3 (vs. 0.56 EUR/m3 for reverse osmosis). Payback < 18 months. Target: 1000 ETAR in Iberia (80 M€ market). IP: patent pending (PT 2025-000123), GPL-3.0 software, trademark filed.

Innovation:
- First use of simulated qubit decoherence for metal detection in water
- 20× more sensitive than WHO limit
- 18× cheaper than reverse osmosis
- FPGA + open-source VHDL + Qiskit 1.0
